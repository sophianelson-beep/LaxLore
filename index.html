From Indigenous Roots to Global Game

Origins Evolution Impact Analysis

Origins → Where/How Did It Start?

Women's lacrosse originates from Indigenous North American traditions, especially the Haudenosaunee. The original game was deeply spiritual and cultural, often played to resolve conflicts and strengthen communities.

In 1890, :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} adapted the sport at Smith College, creating a structured version for women that emphasized skill over contact.

Change Over Time → How Has It Evolved?

1890s: Creation of official rules by Berenson, making the sport accessible in schools.
1930s–1970s: Expansion into Europe and formation of international competition.
1990s–Today: NCAA recognition and rapid global growth.

The sport evolved in equipment, athleticism, and strategy while maintaining its non-contact identity.
